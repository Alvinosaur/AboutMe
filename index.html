Robotic Air Hockey

Summary

My Awesome Team Members:
Hojun Byun: Mechanical, Motion Planning
Joseph Chang: Mechanical, Electrical
Venkata Thallam: Computer Vision, Motion Planning
Yang Yue: Computer Vision
Kevin Wang: Computer Vision

For CMU's weeklong hackathon, Build18, our team built a vision-guided, robotic airhockey arm. Hoping to avoid the typical human v.s single arm that has been done before, we instead imagined up a self-learning system with two collaborating arms that adjusts from its mistakes. Given only a week, we set the basic system as our first milestone.

System at a Glance

My Contributions

Arm Trajectory Planning System Integration Motor Controls Mechanical Arm Design

Rather use existing packages, I decided to calculate the equations for arm movement from scratch using physics and matrices. The hardest part was to calculate the desired rotation of the two arm joints to drive the end effector's motion. We wrote all code with enough modularity to run on both the main system and in simulators(shown on the right) without any changes. Checkout details of the calculations!

One of my main objectives was to learn Robot Operating System (ROS), which simplifies communication of data between difference processes. ROS simplified communication of our custom messages between the brain of our system, a Raspberry Pi, and the arm controller, an Arduino. We also created ROS services to calibrate the computer vision to any puck color in any lighting condition.

We used stepper motors for their accurate position-tracking and high torque. Modifying an existing API(AccelStepper), we were able to have the motor accelerate to the end position and only after begin to decelerate.

We designed a belt driver to control the two joints of our 2-Degree of Freedom arm. Initially, one would think to directly connect the motors to each of the arm's joints. The mass of the motor on the 2nd joint, however, would create high inertia and slow down the arm. With the 2nd motor attached to the base and controlling the 2nd joint with a lightweight belt.
